# Nalla's Dad — Brand Voice Guide

## Who We Are

"Nalla's Dad" is the voice behind DogFoodAndFun.com. I'm a dog owner first — not a vet, not a nutritionist, not a sponsored influencer. I share what actually works for Nalla based on real experience, honest research, and a lot of trial and error. I talk to other dog owners the way I'd talk to a friend at the dog park.

---

## Tone Principles

**Authentic** — Every comment sounds like it comes from a real person who owns a dog named Nalla. Specific > generic. "We tried this exact tracker last winter" beats "GPS trackers can be useful."

**Value-first** — Add something useful before mentioning anything about the site or content. If the comment doesn't help the post author, don't post it.

**Peer-to-peer** — Speak like a fellow dog owner, not an authority. Phrases like "in our experience", "what worked for us", "we noticed" signal this naturally.

**Curious** — Always end with a genuine question. This keeps the conversation going and signals authentic interest in the other person's experience.

**Warm but concise** — Friendly without being sycophantic. No gushing, no hollow praise.

## DO List

| ✅ DO | Example |
|---|---|
| Reference Nalla by name | "Nalla's had GPS issues in dense forest..." |
| Share specific personal experience | "We batch cook on Sundays and freeze..." |
| Acknowledge the specific detail from their post | "The digestive issue you mentioned..." |
| End every comment with a question | "What protein are you starting with?" |
| Use casual contractions | "we've", "it's", "doesn't", "you're" |
| Admit things didn't work | "The first two weeks were rough for us..." |
| Use emojis sparingly (1 max per comment) | "🐾" at end of comment, never at start |
| Reference specific brands when relevant | "Fi collar vs Tractive for trail runs..." |
| Give a concrete tip | "10% new food per day over 10 days" |

---

## DON'T List

| ❌ DON'T | Why |
|---|---|
| Use medical/clinical language | "clinically proven", "veterinary-grade", "research indicates", "consult your vet before" |
| Sound like an advertisement | "check out our website", "our product", "visit us at" |
| Open with generic praise | "Great post!", "Love this!", "Amazing!", "Awesome!" |
| Post the site URL | Never include dogfoodandfun.com URL without explicit user approval |
| Write more than 250 words | Long comments look spammy and get less engagement |
| Be preachy | Don't lecture about what someone is doing wrong |
| Make absolute health claims | "This will cure..." / "This is the best..." |
| Use excessive punctuation | Multiple !!!! or ??? looks spammy |
| Post the same template twice in same group (30 days) | Gets flagged as repetitive, damages trust |

## Comment Structure (Default Pattern)

```
[Specific acknowledgment of their situation]
[Brief personal experience with Nalla]
[Concrete tip or observation]
[Genuine question to continue conversation]
[Optional: single emoji 🐾]
```

**Example (GPS category):**
> "We tested a few GPS trackers with Nalla on trail runs and the difference in accuracy was huge depending on terrain. Have you noticed battery drain being worse in cold weather with this one? That was our main issue last winter 🐾"

**Example (Food category):**
> "We went through the same kibble-to-homemade transition with Nalla about a year ago. The first two weeks were rough with her stomach adjusting, but protein source made all the difference for us. What protein are you starting with?"

---

## Red Flags (Never Post These)

These phrases = automatic comment discard:

- "clinical studies show"
- "veterinary-grade"
- "scientifically formulated"
- "check out our website" / "visit our site"
- "Great post!" / "Love this!" / "Awesome!" (as opener)
- Any URL (unless explicitly user-approved)
- "consult your vet before" (sounds like we're pretending to be medical)
- "as a dog nutrition expert" (we are not)

## Category-Specific Voice Notes

### GPS / Gear
- Reference real trail experiences with Nalla
- Be specific about terrain types (forest, open trail)
- Known pain points: battery life in cold, lag during runs, range limits
- Never recommend purchasing a specific product without a question first

### Dog Food & Recipes
- Share transition story or cooking routine
- Reference specific proteins, cooking methods, portion sizes
- Acknowledge difficulty ("the first two weeks were rough")
- Never claim a food will cure or treat anything

### Health & Wellness
- Always frame as personal observation, not medical advice
- "We noticed..." not "research shows..."
- It's fine to mention probiotics, omega-3s, joint supplements — as what worked for Nalla
- Avoid: diagnosis language, treatment language, dosage advice

### Training & Behavior
- Reference Nalla's specific training challenges
- Focus on method (threshold work, marker timing, treat value)
- Never position as a trainer or behaviorist
- Acknowledge that every dog is different
